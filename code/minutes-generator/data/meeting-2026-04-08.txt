Topic: DPDP meeting
\ discussion of DPDP in the same room after DPVCG meeting agenda

Beatriz: consent manager
Ajay: consent management, consent holder (citizen who is giving consent) can switch from one consent manager to another, consent receipt should be portable i.e. move from one manager to another, citizens are free to use any consent manager
Ajay: the second thing is that consent records and receipts should be interoperable; which is the law; but the law does not prescribe a specific standard -- just that it has to be compatible; so it relies on the implementations. Goal is to have a standard for interoperable consent records and receipts.
Harsh: we have work on ISO 27560 which helps with this; and we are also involved in the revision of this standard; and we have a DPV implementation guide for consent records -- so we need to create a guide for the requirements of DPDP
Art: Don't have a specific focus but want to learn the process as I want to do similar things for the Thai data protection law; specifically those parts that concern cross-border data transfer
Harsh: what does your organisation do for DPDP (to Ajay)
Ajay: we are not an official consent manager as approved by the govt, but we build consent management solutions for our clients
